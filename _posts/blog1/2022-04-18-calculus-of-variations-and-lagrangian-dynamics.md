Consider $ L(q, \dot{q}) $

Then,

$$
    dL = \frac{\partial L}{ \partial q} dq + \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}} d\dot(q)
$$

Define quantities $ p, \dot(p) $ such that, 

$$ \frac{\partial L}{\partial q}  = \dot{p} \\
  \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}} = p
$$
